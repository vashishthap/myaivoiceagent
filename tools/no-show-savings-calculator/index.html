How to fix it: confirmations, deposits, AI follow-ups

No-shows aren't one problem. They're three, and each one wants its own intervention. Most UK practices we work with run all three in parallel within a few weeks of going live.

  1. Pre-appointment confirmations — an SMS the day before and a short outbound AI call the morning of. The combination typically drops no-show rates from 8–12% to 4–6%, because patients with second thoughts cancel rather than ghost. A cancel you know about by 9am is a slot you can refill by 11am; a cancel you find out about at the chair is dead time.
  2. Deposit-taking on high-value slots — for new private patients, complex restorative work, and longer hygiene appointments, a £25–£50 holding deposit cuts no-show rates dramatically. The agent can take the deposit on the booking call, refund it on attendance, or apply it to the bill.
  3. Automated rebooking from a waitlist — when a slot opens, the AI works a prioritised short-list of patients (anyone who asked for an earlier date, anyone overdue for recall) and offers the slot via outbound call or WhatsApp. Same-day refill rates of 40–60% are realistic with a working waitlist of 20–30 names.

For most UK dental practices, the three together convert £3,000–£8,000 of monthly no-show losses into £1,500–£5,000 of recovered revenue — with no extra reception headcount. The full setup is what we run on every dental practice deployment, and the same logic underlies the broader missed-call cost calculator — an unanswered booking call and an unfilled slot are two sides of the same problem.
