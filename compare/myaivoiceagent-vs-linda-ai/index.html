Where Linda is better (dental-only depth)

Linda earned its reputation honestly. If your business is "private dental practice", three things put Linda ahead of almost anyone in the UK category — including us.

1. In-call deposit collection

Linda will take a deposit on the same call where the patient is booking — a £20 to £50 hold against a hygienist slot, an implant consultation, or a high-value cosmetic appointment. For a private practice with a no-show problem, this is genuinely a one-feature buying decision. We deliberately do this differently: we send a Stripe checkout link via SMS or WhatsApp immediately after the call so the card details never sit on the call recording or pass through any AI provider's infrastructure. Both approaches reduce no-shows. Linda's is more integrated. Ours is more PCI-conservative and easier to defend to a data-protection officer.

2. Polished private-dental persona

Linda's voice and call-flow defaults are tuned for the kind of practice that sells implants, Invisalign, and cosmetic packages — confident, warm, ever-so-slightly upmarket. Out of the box, that persona suits a high-street private practice better than a generic multi-vertical default. We calibrate per industry as well, but Linda's private-dental polish is real and worth recognising.

3. Deep, dental-only roadmap

A vendor that only does one vertical can ship features specific to that vertical faster than a multi-vertical operator. Linda's roadmap moves on dental-specific items first — payment plans, lab-stage tracking, recall campaigns. If you only run dental practices, that focus is a feature, not a constraint. We split engineering attention across four verticals; Linda doesn't.

The nuance: voice is a tie

Both products use top-tier voice synthesis and both sound British. Linda has the polish edge for private dental; we have the calibration breadth for non-dental. In blind A/B tests with UK callers in May 2026, both clear the "is this a real person?" bar comfortably.

Where we're better

Five places we believe MyAIVoiceAgent earns the comparison.

1. Transparent, published pricing

We publish three flat tiers on /pricing: £200 Starter, £350 Pro, £500 Premier, plus a one-time £500–£1,000 setup. Linda asks you to book a sales call to get a number. For a busy practice manager who just wants to model 12-month cost across three vendors before deciding which two to demo, that opacity is a real friction. Transparent pricing isn't only honesty — it's faster procurement.

2. NHS-mixed dental, out of the box

A UK dental practice taking NHS patients needs a receptionist who understands UDA bands, recall logic, NHS 111 handover, and urgent dental care criteria. Our dental call flow ships with all of this pre-built and validated against real UK NHS guidance. Linda is fully capable of NHS-mixed work, but its product DNA leans private. For a heavily NHS-weighted practice, ask both vendors to demonstrate a Band 2 emergency triage live on a call. See our dental lander for the full NHS flow we ship.

3. Multi-vertical breadth (one stack, four industries)

A growing portfolio business often runs more than one kind of operation — a dental group that also owns a clinic finance brand, a family office that holds salons and restaurants, a multi-site trades operator with a body-shop sister business. Linda's dental focus means each non-dental sister business needs a separate vendor. We handle dental, salon, restaurant, and trades from one stack with one contract, one DPA, and one invoice. For an operator with five sites and three verticals, that consolidation alone pays for the platform.

4. PCI-conservative payment flow

Taking card details on a recorded AI call has to be defended carefully under PCI DSS and ICO guidance. Our decision to push payment to an SMS or WhatsApp Stripe link keeps card data entirely off the AI provider's infrastructure and out of the call recording. For a practice with a strict data protection officer or a private equity backer doing security diligence, that architecture is easier to sign off than in-call card capture. Different reasonable answers — but worth knowing the trade-off before you sign.

5. UK-built, UK-supported, flat fee

Both products are UK-friendly. We're UK-built, UK-staffed, and our flat-fee model means a busy month in March doesn't trigger a surprise bill in April. For a practice manager who's been burned by per-minute billing on a previous provider, a published flat fee with a clear call-volume cap is the procurement answer that doesn't require a quarterly review.
